1. Differentiation rules, specifically the product rule and the power rule.
2. The ability to evaluate a function and its derivative at a specific point.

Detailed Solution:

First, we need to find the derivative of $f(x)$ using the product rule, which states that if $f(x) = g(x)h(x)$, then $f'(x) = g'(x)h(x) + g(x)h'(x)$.

Let $g(x) = x^2 + 1$ and $h(x) = 3x^2 - x$.

Using the power rule, $g'(x) = 2x$ and $h'(x) = 6x - 1$.

Now, we can find $f'(x)$:
\begin{align*}
f'(x) &= g'(x)h(x) + g(x)h'(x) \\
&= (2x)(3x^2 - x) + (x^2 + 1)(6x - 1) \\
&= 6x^3 - 2x^2 + 6x^3 - x^2 + 6x - 1 \\
&= 12x^3 - 3x^2 + 6x - 1.
\end{align*}

Now that we have $f'(x)$, we can find $f'(1)$ by substituting $x = 1$:
\begin{align*}
f'(1) &= 12(1)^3 - 3(1)^2 + 6(1) - 1 \\
&= 12 - 3 + 6 - 1 \\
&= 14.
\end{align*}

Therefore, the value of $f'(1)$ is $14$.

Final Answer: $14$
