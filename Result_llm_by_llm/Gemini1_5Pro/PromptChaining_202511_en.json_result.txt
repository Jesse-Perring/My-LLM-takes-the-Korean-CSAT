Problem: Given the polynomial function $f(x)$ such that for all real $x$, $$ \int_0^x f(t) dt = 3x^3 + 2x $$, what is the value of $f(1)$?
Solution:
{
    Analysis(step1): 
* **Problem Type:** Calculus - Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

* **Key Concepts:**
    * **Definite Integral:** The problem involves a definite integral of the function $f(t)$ from 0 to $x$.
    * **Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part 1):**  This theorem states that if $f$ is continuous on $[a, b]$ and $F(x) = \int_a^x f(t) dt$, then $F'(x) = f(x)$ for all $x$ in $(a, b)$.  This is the core concept needed to solve the problem.
    * **Derivative of a Polynomial:**  The problem requires taking the derivative of a polynomial.

* **Approach Strategies:**

1. **Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:** The problem gives us $\int_0^x f(t) dt = 3x^3 + 2x$.  Let $F(x) = \int_0^x f(t) dt$.  Then, according to the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, $F'(x) = f(x)$.

2. **Differentiate the Polynomial:**  We have $F(x) = 3x^3 + 2x$.  Differentiate this with respect to $x$ to find $F'(x)$.
