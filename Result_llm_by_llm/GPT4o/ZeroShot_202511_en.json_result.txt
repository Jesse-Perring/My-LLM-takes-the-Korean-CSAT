문제 1:
To solve the problem, we need to evaluate the expression \(\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}\).

First, let's express each term using exponents:
\[
\sqrt[3]{5} = 5^{\frac{1}{3}}
\]
\[
25^{\frac{1}{3}} = (5^2)^{\frac{1}{3}}
\]

Using the property of exponents \((a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n}\), we can simplify \(25^{\frac{1}{3}}\):
\[
(5^2)^{\frac{1}{3}} = 5^{2 \cdot \frac{1}{3}} = 5^{\frac{2}{3}}
\]

Now, substitute these back into the original expression:
\[
\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}} = 5^{\frac{1}{3}} \times 5^{\frac{2}{3}}
\]

Using the property of exponents \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\), we combine the exponents:
\[
5^{\frac{1}{3}} \times 5^{\frac{2}{3}} = 5^{\frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{3}} = 5^{\frac{3}{3}} = 5^1 = 5
\]

Thus, the value of the expression is \(\boxed{5}\).

문제 2:
To find the value of \(\lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(2+h)-f(2)}{h}\), we need to evaluate the derivative of the function \(f(x) = x^3 - 8x + 7\) at \(x = 2\). The expression \(\lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(2+h)-f(2)}{h}\) is the definition of the derivative of \(f\) at \(x = 2\).

First, we calculate \(f(2)\):

\[
f(2) = 2^3 - 8 \times 2 + 7 = 8 - 16 + 7 = -1
\]

Next, we calculate \(f(2+h)\):

\[
f(2+h) = (2+h)^3 - 8(2+h) + 7
\]

Expanding \((2+h)^3\) using the binomial theorem:

\[
(2+h)^3 = 2^3 + 3 \cdot 2^2 \cdot h + 3 \cdot 2 \cdot h^2 + h^3 = 8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

Substituting back into the expression for \(f(2+h)\):

\[
f(2+h) = (8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3) - 8(2+h) + 7
\]

Simplifying the expression:

\[
= 8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3 - 16 - 8h + 7
\]

Combine like terms:

\[
= (8 - 16 + 7) + (12h - 8h) + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

\[
= -1 + 4h + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

Now, substitute \(f(2+h)\) and \(f(2)\) into the difference quotient:

\[
\frac{f(2+h) - f(2)}{h} = \frac{(-1 + 4h + 6h^2 + h^3) - (-1)}{h}
\]

Simplify the numerator:

\[
= \frac{-1 + 4h + 6h^2 + h^3 + 1}{h} = \frac{4h + 6h^2 + h^3}{h}
\]

Factor out \(h\) from the numerator:

\[
= \frac{h(4 + 6h + h^2)}{h}
\]

Cancel \(h\) from the numerator and denominator:

\[
= 4 + 6h + h^2
\]

Finally, take the limit as \(h \to 0\):

\[
\lim_{h \to 0} (4 + 6h + h^2) = 4
\]

Thus, the value of \(\lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(2+h)-f(2)}{h}\) is \(\boxed{4}\).

\[
a_n = a_1 \cdot k^{n-1}

Thus, the terms \( a_1 \), \( a_2 \), and \( a_4 \) can be expressed as:

\[
a_1 = k
\]
\[
a_2 = k \cdot k = k^2

\[
a_4 = k \cdot k^3 = k^4
\]

We are given the equation:

\[
\frac{a_4}{a_2} + \frac{a_2}{a_1} = 30

\[

\]
\[

\]

This simplifies to the quadratic equation:

\[
k^2 + k - 30 = 0
\]

To solve this quadratic equation, we use the quadratic formula:

\[
k = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}
\]

where \( a = 1 \), \( b = 1 \), and \( c = -30 \). Plugging in these values, we have:

\[

5x + a & \text{if } x < -2 \\
x^2 - a & \text{if } x \geq -2 
\end{cases}
\]

For continuity at \( x = -2 \), the left-hand limit as \( x \to -2^- \) must equal the right-hand limit as \( x \to -2^+ \), and both must equal \( f(-2) \).

1. **Left-hand limit** as \( x \to -2^- \):
   \[
   \lim_{x \to -2^-} f(x) = \lim_{x \to -2^-} (5x + a) = 5(-2) + a = -10 + a
   \]

   \[

   \]

For continuity at \( x = -2 \), set the left-hand limit equal to the right-hand limit:
\[
-10 + a = 4 - a
\]

Solve for \( a \):
\[
-10 + a = 4 - a
\]
\[
2a = 14
\]
\[

\]

Thus, the value of the constant \( a \) that makes \( f(x) \) continuous for all real numbers is \(\boxed{7}\).

문제 5:
To find \( f'(1) \), we first need to find the derivative of the function \( f(x) = (x^2 + 1)(3x^2 - x) \).

We will use the product rule for differentiation, which states that if \( u(x) \) and \( v(x) \) are functions of \( x \), then the derivative of their product \( u(x)v(x) \) is given by:

\[
(uv)' = u'v + uv'
\]

Let \( u(x) = x^2 + 1 \) and \( v(x) = 3x^2 - x \).

First, we find the derivatives \( u'(x) \) and \( v'(x) \):

\[
u'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(x^2 + 1) = 2x
\]

\[
v'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(3x^2 - x) = 6x - 1
\]

Now, apply the product rule:

\[
f'(x) = u'(x)v(x) + u(x)v'(x)
\]

Substitute \( u(x) \), \( u'(x) \), \( v(x) \), and \( v'(x) \) into the product rule:

\[
f'(x) = (2x)(3x^2 - x) + (x^2 + 1)(6x - 1)
\]

Simplify each term:

1. \( (2x)(3x^2 - x) = 2x \cdot 3x^2 - 2x \cdot x = 6x^3 - 2x^2 \)

2. \( (x^2 + 1)(6x - 1) = x^2 \cdot 6x + 1 \cdot 6x - x^2 \cdot 1 - 1 \cdot 1 \)
   \[
   = 6x^3 + 6x - x^2 - 1
   \]

Combine the expressions:

\[
f'(x) = (6x^3 - 2x^2) + (6x^3 + 6x - x^2 - 1)
\]

Combine like terms:

\[
f'(x) = 6x^3 + 6x^3 - 2x^2 - x^2 + 6x - 1
\]

\[
= 12x^3 - 3x^2 + 6x - 1
\]

Now, evaluate \( f'(1) \):

\[
f'(1) = 12(1)^3 - 3(1)^2 + 6(1) - 1
\]

\[
= 12 \cdot 1 - 3 \cdot 1 + 6 \cdot 1 - 1
\]

\[
= 12 - 3 + 6 - 1
\]

\[
= 14
\]

Thus, the value of \( f'(1) \) is \(\boxed{14}\).

To solve the problem, we start by using the trigonometric identity for cosine of a sum:

\[
\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2} + \theta\right) = \cos\frac{\pi}{2}\cos\theta - \sin\frac{\pi}{2}\sin\theta = 0 \cdot \cos\theta - 1 \cdot \sin\theta = -\sin\theta.
\]

Given that \(\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2} + \theta\right) = -\frac{1}{5}\), we equate this to the expression we derived:

\[
-\sin\theta = -\frac{1}{5}.
\]

Thus, we have:

\[
\sin\theta = \frac{1}{5}.
\]

Next, we need to find \(\frac{\sin\theta}{1-\cos^2\theta}\). We start by using the Pythagorean identity:

\[
\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1.
\]

Substituting \(\sin\theta = \frac{1}{5}\), we find \(\sin^2\theta\):

\[
\sin^2\theta = \left(\frac{1}{5}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{25}.
\]

Substitute this into the Pythagorean identity:

\[
\frac{1}{25} + \cos^2\theta = 1.
\]

Solving for \(\cos^2\theta\), we have:

\[
\cos^2\theta = 1 - \frac{1}{25} = \frac{25}{25} - \frac{1}{25} = \frac{24}{25}.
\]

Now, substitute \(\sin\theta\) and \(\cos^2\theta\) into the expression \(\frac{\sin\theta}{1-\cos^2\theta}\):

\[
\frac{\sin\theta}{1-\cos^2\theta} = \frac{\frac{1}{5}}{1 - \frac{24}{25}}.
\]

Calculate the denominator:

\[
1 - \frac{24}{25} = \frac{25}{25} - \frac{24}{25} = \frac{1}{25}.
\]

Thus, the expression becomes:

\[
\frac{\frac{1}{5}}{\frac{1}{25}} = \frac{1}{5} \times \frac{25}{1} = \frac{25}{5} = 5.
\]

Therefore, the value of \(\frac{\sin\theta}{1-\cos^2\theta}\) is \(\boxed{5}\).

\[
F(x) = \int_0^x f(t) \, dt = 3x^3 + 2x

문제 8:
To solve for \( a \times b \), we first need to simplify the expressions for \( a \) and \( b \).

Starting with \( a \):
\[
a = 2 \log \frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} + \log_2 20
\]

First, simplify \( 2 \log \frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} \):
\[
\log \frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} = \log 10^{-\frac{1}{2}} = -\frac{1}{2} \log 10
\]
Thus,
\[
2 \log \frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} = 2 \times -\frac{1}{2} \log 10 = -\log 10
\]

Next, simplify \( \log_2 20 \):
\[
\log_2 20 = \log_2 (2^2 \times 5) = \log_2 2^2 + \log_2 5 = 2 + \log_2 5
\]

Now, substitute these into the expression for \( a \):
\[
a = -\log 10 + 2 + \log_2 5
\]

We know that:
\[
\log 10 = \log_{10} 10 = 1
\]
Thus:
\[
a = -1 + 2 + \log_2 5 = 1 + \log_2 5
\]

Now, consider \( b \):
\[
b = \log 2
\]

We need to find \( a \times b \):
\[
a \times b = (1 + \log_2 5) \times \log 2
\]

Distribute the multiplication:
\[
a \times b = \log 2 + \log_2 5 \cdot \log 2
\]

To simplify \( \log_2 5 \cdot \log 2 \), use the change of base formula:
\[
\log_2 5 = \frac{\log 5}{\log 2}
\]
Thus:
\[
\log_2 5 \cdot \log 2 = \frac{\log 5}{\log 2} \cdot \log 2 = \log 5
\]

Therefore:
\[
a \times b = \log 2 + \log 5
\]

Using the property of logarithms that \(\log a + \log b = \log (a \times b)\):
\[
a \times b = \log (2 \times 5) = \log 10
\]

Since \(\log 10 = 1\), we have:
\[
a \times b = 1
\]

Thus, the value of \( a \times b \) is \(\boxed{1}\).

\]

\]
\[
t + 1 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = -1
\]

Next, we find the acceleration \( a(t) \) of point P, which is the derivative of the velocity function \( v(t) \):
