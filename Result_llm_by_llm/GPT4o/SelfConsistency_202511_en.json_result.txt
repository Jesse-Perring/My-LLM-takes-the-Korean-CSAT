Solution:
To solve the problem of finding the value of \(\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}\), we can use properties of exponents to simplify the expression.

1. **Convert to Exponent Notation:**
   - The cube root of 5 can be expressed as an exponent: \(\sqrt[3]{5} = 5^{\frac{1}{3}}\).
   - Similarly, \(25^{\frac{1}{3}}\) can be rewritten using the fact that \(25 = 5^2\): 
     \[
     25^{\frac{1}{3}} = (5^2)^{\frac{1}{3}}
     \]

2. **Simplify Using Exponent Rules:**
   - Apply the power of a power property \((a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n}\) to simplify \( (5^2)^{\frac{1}{3}} \):
     \[
     (5^2)^{\frac{1}{3}} = 5^{2 \cdot \frac{1}{3}} = 5^{\frac{2}{3}}
     \]

3. **Combine the Expressions:**
   - Now, the original expression \(\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}\) becomes:
     \[
     5^{\frac{1}{3}} \times 5^{\frac{2}{3}}
     \]

4. **Use the Product of Powers Property:**
   - The property \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\) allows us to combine the exponents:
     \[
     5^{\frac{1}{3}} \times 5^{\frac{2}{3}} = 5^{\frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{3}}
     \]

5. **Add the Exponents:**
   - Calculate the sum of the exponents:
     \[
     \frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{3} = \frac{1+2}{3} = \frac{3}{3} = 1
     \]

6. **Simplify the Expression:**
   - The expression simplifies to:
     \[
     5^1 = 5
     \]

Therefore, the value of \(\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}\) is \(5\).

**Answer:** 5

Solution:
To solve the problem, we need to find the derivative of the function \( f(x) = x^3 - 8x + 7 \) at the point \( x = 2 \). The derivative of a function at a point is given by the limit:

\[
f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}
\]

We are asked to find \( f'(2) \), which is:

\[
f'(2) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(2+h) - f(2)}{h}
\]

First, calculate \( f(2) \):

\[
f(2) = 2^3 - 8 \times 2 + 7 = 8 - 16 + 7 = -1
\]

Next, calculate \( f(2+h) \):

\[
f(2+h) = (2+h)^3 - 8(2+h) + 7
\]

Expand \( (2+h)^3 \):

\[
(2+h)^3 = 2^3 + 3 \cdot 2^2 \cdot h + 3 \cdot 2 \cdot h^2 + h^3 = 8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

Substitute back into \( f(2+h) \):

\[
f(2+h) = (8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3) - 8(2+h) + 7
\]

Simplify \( -8(2+h) \):

\[
-8(2+h) = -16 - 8h
\]

Thus, \( f(2+h) \) becomes:

\[
f(2+h) = 8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3 - 16 - 8h + 7
\]

Combine like terms:

\[
f(2+h) = (8 - 16 + 7) + (12h - 8h) + 6h^2 + h^3 = -1 + 4h + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

Now, compute \( f(2+h) - f(2) \):

\[
f(2+h) - f(2) = (-1 + 4h + 6h^2 + h^3) - (-1) = 4h + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

Substitute into the limit expression:

\[
\frac{f(2+h) - f(2)}{h} = \frac{4h + 6h^2 + h^3}{h}
\]

Simplify by dividing each term by \( h \):

\[
= \frac{4h}{h} + \frac{6h^2}{h} + \frac{h^3}{h} = 4 + 6h + h^2
\]

Take the limit as \( h \to 0 \):

\[
\lim_{h \to 0} (4 + 6h + h^2) = 4 + 6 \cdot 0 + 0^2 = 4
\]

Therefore, the value of the limit is:

\[
\boxed{4}
\]

This result represents the derivative of the function \( f(x) \) at \( x = 2 \), which is the slope of the tangent line to the curve at that point.
Answer: 4

\[
k^2 + k - 30 = 0

\[

To solve the problem of finding \( f'(1) \) for the function \( f(x) = (x^2 + 1)(3x^2 - x) \), we need to use the product rule for differentiation. The product rule states that if a function \( f(x) \) is the product of two functions \( u(x) \) and \( v(x) \), then its derivative is given by:

\[
f'(x) = u'(x)v(x) + u(x)v'(x)
\]

For the given function, let's identify:
- \( u(x) = x^2 + 1 \)
- \( v(x) = 3x^2 - x \)

First, we calculate the derivatives of \( u(x) \) and \( v(x) \):
- The derivative of \( u(x) = x^2 + 1 \) is \( u'(x) = 2x \).
- The derivative of \( v(x) = 3x^2 - x \) is \( v'(x) = 6x - 1 \).

Now, applying the product rule, we find \( f'(x) \):
\[
f'(x) = u'(x)v(x) + u(x)v'(x)
\]
\[
f'(x) = (2x)(3x^2 - x) + (x^2 + 1)(6x - 1)
\]

Next, we expand each term:
1. \( (2x)(3x^2 - x) = 2x \cdot 3x^2 - 2x \cdot x = 6x^3 - 2x^2 \)
2. \( (x^2 + 1)(6x - 1) = x^2 \cdot 6x + 1 \cdot 6x - x^2 \cdot 1 - 1 \cdot 1 = 6x^3 + 6x - x^2 - 1 \)

Combine these results:
\[
f'(x) = (6x^3 - 2x^2) + (6x^3 + 6x - x^2 - 1)
\]

Combine like terms:
- \( 6x^3 + 6x^3 = 12x^3 \)
- \( -2x^2 - x^2 = -3x^2 \)
- \( 6x \) remains as is
- \( -1 \) remains as is

Thus, the derivative is:
\[
f'(x) = 12x^3 - 3x^2 + 6x - 1
\]

Finally, we evaluate \( f'(1) \):
\[
f'(1) = 12(1)^3 - 3(1)^2 + 6(1) - 1
\]
\[
f'(1) = 12 \cdot 1 - 3 \cdot 1 + 6 \cdot 1 - 1
\]
\[
f'(1) = 12 - 3 + 6 - 1
\]
\[
f'(1) = 14
\]

Therefore, the value of \( f'(1) \) is \(\boxed{14}\).

F'(x) = f(x).

\[
\frac{d}{dx}(3x^3) = 9x^2.

\[
\frac{d}{dx}(2x) = 2.

\[
F'(x) = 9x^2 + 2.

   \[
   \log_2 5 = \frac{\log 5}{\log 2}

   \[

   \[

   \[

   \[
   a \times b = \log 2 + \log 5

   \[
