문제 1:
To solve the problem, we need to evaluate the expression \(\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}\).

First, let's rewrite the expression using exponent notation:
\[
\sqrt[3]{5} = 5^{\frac{1}{3}}
\]
\[
25^{\frac{1}{3}} = (5^2)^{\frac{1}{3}}
\]

Using the property of exponents \((a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n}\), we can simplify \(25^{\frac{1}{3}}\) as follows:
\[
(5^2)^{\frac{1}{3}} = 5^{2 \cdot \frac{1}{3}} = 5^{\frac{2}{3}}
\]

Now, substitute these back into the original expression:
\[
\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}} = 5^{\frac{1}{3}} \times 5^{\frac{2}{3}}
\]

When multiplying expressions with the same base, we add the exponents:
\[
5^{\frac{1}{3}} \times 5^{\frac{2}{3}} = 5^{\frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{3}} = 5^{\frac{3}{3}} = 5^1 = 5
\]

Thus, the value of the expression \(\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}\) is \(\boxed{5}\).

문제 2:
To solve the problem, we need to find the derivative of the function \( f(x) = x^3 - 8x + 7 \) at \( x = 2 \). The expression given, \(\lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(2+h)-f(2)}{h}\), represents the definition of the derivative of \( f \) at \( x = 2 \).

First, let's compute \( f(2) \):
\[
f(2) = 2^3 - 8 \times 2 + 7 = 8 - 16 + 7 = -1.
\]

Next, we need to compute \( f(2+h) \):
\[
f(2+h) = (2+h)^3 - 8(2+h) + 7.
\]

Expanding \( (2+h)^3 \):
\[
(2+h)^3 = 2^3 + 3 \cdot 2^2 \cdot h + 3 \cdot 2 \cdot h^2 + h^3 = 8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3.
\]

Now substitute back into \( f(2+h) \):
\[
f(2+h) = (8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3) - 8(2+h) + 7.
\]

Simplify \( -8(2+h) \):
\[
-8(2+h) = -16 - 8h.
\]

Thus, \( f(2+h) \) becomes:
\[
f(2+h) = 8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3 - 16 - 8h + 7.
\]

Combine like terms:
\[
f(2+h) = (8 - 16 + 7) + (12h - 8h) + 6h^2 + h^3 = -1 + 4h + 6h^2 + h^3.
\]

Now, compute \( f(2+h) - f(2) \):
\[
f(2+h) - f(2) = (-1 + 4h + 6h^2 + h^3) - (-1) = 4h + 6h^2 + h^3.
\]

We need to evaluate the limit:
\[
\lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(2+h) - f(2)}{h} = \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{4h + 6h^2 + h^3}{h}.
\]

Simplify the expression by dividing each term by \( h \):
\[
\frac{4h + 6h^2 + h^3}{h} = 4 + 6h + h^2.
\]

Now, take the limit as \( h \to 0 \):
\[
\lim_{h \rightarrow 0} (4 + 6h + h^2) = 4 + 6 \cdot 0 + 0^2 = 4.
\]

Therefore, the value of the limit is \(\boxed{4}\).

문제 3:
To solve the problem, we need to analyze the given geometric sequence \(\{a_n\}\) where both the first term \(a_1\) and the common ratio are equal to \(k\). The terms of the sequence can be expressed as follows:

\[
a_1 = k, \quad a_2 = k^2, \quad a_3 = k^3, \quad a_4 = k^4, \quad \ldots
\]

We are given the equation:

\[
\frac{a_4}{a_2} + \frac{a_2}{a_1} = 30
\]

First, calculate \(\frac{a_4}{a_2}\):

\[
\frac{a_4}{a_2} = \frac{k^4}{k^2} = k^{4-2} = k^2
\]

Next, calculate \(\frac{a_2}{a_1}\):

\[
\frac{a_2}{a_1} = \frac{k^2}{k} = k^{2-1} = k
\]

Substitute these expressions into the given equation:

\[
k^2 + k = 30
\]

This simplifies to the quadratic equation:

\[
k^2 + k - 30 = 0
\]

To solve this quadratic equation, we use the quadratic formula:

\[
k = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}
\]

where \(a = 1\), \(b = 1\), and \(c = -30\). Calculate the discriminant:

\[
b^2 - 4ac = 1^2 - 4 \times 1 \times (-30) = 1 + 120 = 121
\]

The square root of the discriminant is:

\[
\sqrt{121} = 11
\]

Substitute back into the quadratic formula:

\[
k = \frac{-1 \pm 11}{2}
\]

This gives two potential solutions for \(k\):

\[
k = \frac{-1 + 11}{2} = \frac{10}{2} = 5
\]

\[
k = \frac{-1 - 11}{2} = \frac{-12}{2} = -6
\]

Since \(k\) is a positive number, we select \(k = 5\).

Thus, the value of \(k\) is \(\boxed{5}\).

The function \( f(x) \) is defined as:
\[
f(x) = 
\begin{cases} 
5x + a & \text{if } x < -2 \\ 
x^2 - a & \text{if } x \geq -2 
\end{cases}
\]

For \( f(x) \) to be continuous at \( x = -2 \), the left-hand limit as \( x \) approaches \(-2\), the right-hand limit as \( x \) approaches \(-2\), and the value of the function at \( x = -2 \) must all be equal. 

1. **Left-hand limit as \( x \to -2^- \):**
   \[
   \lim_{x \to -2^-} f(x) = \lim_{x \to -2^-} (5x + a) = 5(-2) + a = -10 + a
   \]

2. **Right-hand limit as \( x \to -2^+ \):**
   \[
   \lim_{x \to -2^+} f(x) = \lim_{x \to -2^+} (x^2 - a) = (-2)^2 - a = 4 - a
   \]

3. **Value of the function at \( x = -2 \):**
   \[
   f(-2) = (-2)^2 - a = 4 - a
   \]

For continuity at \( x = -2 \), we require:
\[
\lim_{x \to -2^-} f(x) = \lim_{x \to -2^+} f(x) = f(-2)
\]

This gives us the equation:
\[
-10 + a = 4 - a
\]

Solving for \( a \):
\[
-10 + a = 4 - a
\]
\[
2a = 14
\]
\[
a = 7
\]

Thus, the value of the constant \( a \) that makes \( f(x) \) continuous for all real numbers is \(\boxed{7}\).

문제 5:
To find \( f'(1) \) for the function \( f(x) = (x^2 + 1)(3x^2 - x) \), we first need to differentiate \( f(x) \). The function is a product of two functions, so we will use the product rule for differentiation. The product rule states that if \( u(x) \) and \( v(x) \) are functions of \( x \), then the derivative of their product is given by:

\[
(uv)' = u'v + uv'
\]

Here, let \( u(x) = x^2 + 1 \) and \( v(x) = 3x^2 - x \). We need to find \( u'(x) \) and \( v'(x) \).

First, differentiate \( u(x) \):
\[
u(x) = x^2 + 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad u'(x) = 2x
\]

Next, differentiate \( v(x) \):
\[
v(x) = 3x^2 - x \quad \Rightarrow \quad v'(x) = 6x - 1
\]

Now apply the product rule:
\[
f'(x) = u'(x)v(x) + u(x)v'(x)
\]

Substitute \( u(x) \), \( u'(x) \), \( v(x) \), and \( v'(x) \) into the formula:
\[
f'(x) = (2x)(3x^2 - x) + (x^2 + 1)(6x - 1)
\]

Simplify each term:
1. \( u'(x)v(x) = 2x(3x^2 - x) = 6x^3 - 2x^2 \)
2. \( u(x)v'(x) = (x^2 + 1)(6x - 1) = x^2(6x - 1) + 1(6x - 1) = 6x^3 - x^2 + 6x - 1 \)

Combine these results:
\[
f'(x) = (6x^3 - 2x^2) + (6x^3 - x^2 + 6x - 1)
\]

Combine like terms:
\[
f'(x) = 6x^3 + 6x^3 - 2x^2 - x^2 + 6x - 1 = 12x^3 - 3x^2 + 6x - 1
\]

Now, evaluate \( f'(1) \):
\[
f'(1) = 12(1)^3 - 3(1)^2 + 6(1) - 1
\]

Calculate each term:
\[
= 12 - 3 + 6 - 1
\]

Combine the results:
\[
= 14
\]

Thus, the value of \( f'(1) \) is \(\boxed{14}\).

\[
\cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2} + \theta\right) = -\sin \theta

\[
-\sin \theta = -\frac{1}{5}
\]

This simplifies to:

\[
\sin \theta = \frac{1}{5}
\]

Next, we need to find the value of \(\frac{\sin \theta}{1 - \cos^2 \theta}\). We know from the Pythagorean identity that:

\[
\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1
\]

Substituting \(\sin \theta = \frac{1}{5}\) into the identity, we find:

\[
\left(\frac{1}{5}\right)^2 + \cos^2 \theta = 1
\]

\[
\frac{1}{25} + \cos^2 \theta = 1
\]

Solving for \(\cos^2 \theta\), we get:

\[
\cos^2 \theta = 1 - \frac{1}{25} = \frac{25}{25} - \frac{1}{25} = \frac{24}{25}
\]

Now, we substitute \(\sin \theta = \frac{1}{5}\) and \(\cos^2 \theta = \frac{24}{25}\) into the expression \(\frac{\sin \theta}{1 - \cos^2 \theta}\):

\[
\frac{\sin \theta}{1 - \cos^2 \theta} = \frac{\frac{1}{5}}{1 - \frac{24}{25}}
\]

Simplifying the denominator:

\[
1 - \frac{24}{25} = \frac{25}{25} - \frac{24}{25} = \frac{1}{25}
\]

Thus, the expression becomes:

\[
\frac{\frac{1}{5}}{\frac{1}{25}} = \frac{1}{5} \times \frac{25}{1} = \frac{25}{5} = 5

Therefore, the value of \(\frac{\sin \theta}{1 - \cos^2 \theta}\) is:

\[
\boxed{5}

문제 7:
To solve this problem, we need to determine the function \( f(x) \) given that

\[
\int_0^x f(t) \, dt = 3x^3 + 2x.
\]

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus tells us that if \( F(x) \) is an antiderivative of \( f(x) \), then

\[
\int_0^x f(t) \, dt = F(x) - F(0).
\]

Given that

\[
\int_0^x f(t) \, dt = 3x^3 + 2x,
\]

we can deduce that \( F(x) = 3x^3 + 2x \) since the constant term \( F(0) \) is zero (as there is no constant term in \( 3x^3 + 2x \)).

To find \( f(x) \), we differentiate \( F(x) \):

\[
f(x) = F'(x).
\]

Calculating the derivative, we have:

\[
F(x) = 3x^3 + 2x,
\]

\[
F'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(3x^3 + 2x) = 9x^2 + 2.
\]

Thus, the function \( f(x) \) is \( 9x^2 + 2 \).

Now, we need to find \( f(1) \):

\[
f(1) = 9(1)^2 + 2 = 9 \times 1 + 2 = 9 + 2 = 11.
\]

Therefore, the value of \( f(1) \) is \(\boxed{11}\).

   \[
   \log \frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} = \log 10^{-1/2} = -\frac{1}{2} \log 10.

   \[

   \]

Thus, we have:
\[
a = -\log 10 + 2 + \log_2 5.
\]

Next, let's express \( \log_2 5 \) in terms of base 10 logarithms:
\[

\[

\[ 
b = \log 2.

\[
a \times b = \left(-\log 10 + 2 + \frac{\log 5}{\log 2}\right) \times \log 2.

Distribute \( \log 2 \) across the terms:

Simplify each term:

To solve this problem, we need to analyze the function \( f(x) = a \cos(bx) + 3 \) and use the given conditions to find the minimum value of \( a + b \) for natural numbers \( a \) and \( b \).

\[

\]

\[

\[

### Step 4: Calculate \( a + b \)

\[
a + b = 10 + 6 = 16
\]

Thus, the minimum value of \( a + b \) for the pair of natural numbers \( (a, b) \) is \(\boxed{16}\).

\[

Dividing the entire equation by 3:

\[

\]

This quadratic equation can be factored as:

\[
(t - 2)(t + 1) = 0
\]

Thus, the solutions are:

\[
t = 2 \quad \text{and} \quad t = -1
\]

Since \( t \geq 0 \), we only consider \( t = 2 \).

### Step 3: Calculate the acceleration at \( t = 2 \)

The acceleration \( a(t) \) is the derivative of the velocity function:

\[
a(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(3t^2 - 3t - 6) = 6t - 3
\]

Substitute \( t = 2 \) into the acceleration function:

\[
a(2) = 6(2) - 3 = 12 - 3 = 9
\]

### Conclusion

The acceleration of point P at the time when its direction of motion changes is \(\boxed{9}\).
