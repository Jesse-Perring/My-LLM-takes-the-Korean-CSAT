문제 1:
To solve the problem \(\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}\), we need to simplify the expression step-by-step.

First, let's express each term with exponents:

1. \(\sqrt[3]{5}\) can be written as \(5^{\frac{1}{3}}\).
2. \(25^{\frac{1}{3}}\) is already in the form of an exponent.

Now, the expression becomes:

\[
5^{\frac{1}{3}} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}
\]

Next, notice that \(25\) can be rewritten as \(5^2\). Therefore, \(25^{\frac{1}{3}}\) can be rewritten using the property of exponents \((a^m)^n = a^{m \cdot n}\):

\[
25^{\frac{1}{3}} = (5^2)^{\frac{1}{3}} = 5^{2 \cdot \frac{1}{3}} = 5^{\frac{2}{3}}
\]

Now substitute back into the expression:

\[
5^{\frac{1}{3}} \times 5^{\frac{2}{3}}
\]

Using the property of exponents that states \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\), we can combine the exponents:

\[
5^{\frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{3}} = 5^{\frac{3}{3}} = 5^1
\]

Thus, the expression simplifies to:

\[
5
\]

Therefore, the value of \(\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}\) is \(\boxed{5}\).

문제 2:
To solve the problem, we need to find the derivative of the function \( f(x) = x^3 - 8x + 7 \) at \( x = 2 \). The derivative of a function at a point is given by the limit:

\[
\lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(2+h) - f(2)}{h}
\]

First, calculate \( f(2) \):

\[
f(2) = 2^3 - 8 \times 2 + 7 = 8 - 16 + 7 = -1
\]

Next, calculate \( f(2+h) \):

\[
f(2+h) = (2+h)^3 - 8(2+h) + 7
\]

Expand \( (2+h)^3 \):

\[
(2+h)^3 = 2^3 + 3 \cdot 2^2 \cdot h + 3 \cdot 2 \cdot h^2 + h^3 = 8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

Substitute back into \( f(2+h) \):

\[
f(2+h) = (8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3) - 8(2+h) + 7
\]

Simplify \( -8(2+h) \):

\[
-8(2+h) = -16 - 8h
\]

Combine all terms:

\[
f(2+h) = 8 + 12h + 6h^2 + h^3 - 16 - 8h + 7
\]

Simplify:

\[
f(2+h) = (8 - 16 + 7) + (12h - 8h) + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

\[
f(2+h) = -1 + 4h + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

Now, find \( f(2+h) - f(2) \):

\[
f(2+h) - f(2) = (-1 + 4h + 6h^2 + h^3) - (-1)
\]

\[
f(2+h) - f(2) = 4h + 6h^2 + h^3
\]

Substitute into the limit expression:

\[
\lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(2+h) - f(2)}{h} = \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{4h + 6h^2 + h^3}{h}
\]

Factor out \( h \) from the numerator:

\[
= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{h(4 + 6h + h^2)}{h}
\]

Cancel \( h \) in the numerator and denominator:

\[
= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} (4 + 6h + h^2)
\]

Evaluate the limit as \( h \rightarrow 0 \):

\[
= 4 + 6(0) + 0^2 = 4
\]

Thus, the value of the limit is:

\[
\boxed{4}
\]

문제 4:
To determine the value of the constant \( a \) that makes the function \( f(x) \) continuous for all real numbers, we need to ensure that the left-hand limit and the right-hand limit at the point \( x = -2 \) are equal, and also equal to the function value at \( x = -2 \).

The function \( f(x) \) is defined as:
\[
f(x) = 
\begin{cases} 
5x + a & \text{if } x < -2 \\ 
x^2 - a & \text{if } x \geq -2 
\end{cases}
\]

### Step 1: Calculate the left-hand limit as \( x \to -2^- \)

For \( x < -2 \), the function is given by \( f(x) = 5x + a \). Thus, the left-hand limit as \( x \to -2^- \) is:
\[
\lim_{x \to -2^-} f(x) = \lim_{x \to -2^-} (5x + a) = 5(-2) + a = -10 + a
\]

### Step 2: Calculate the right-hand limit as \( x \to -2^+ \)

For \( x \geq -2 \), the function is given by \( f(x) = x^2 - a \). Thus, the right-hand limit as \( x \to -2^+ \) is:
\[
\lim_{x \to -2^+} f(x) = \lim_{x \to -2^+} (x^2 - a) = (-2)^2 - a = 4 - a
\]

### Step 3: Set the left-hand limit equal to the right-hand limit

For continuity at \( x = -2 \), the left-hand limit must equal the right-hand limit:
\[
-10 + a = 4 - a
\]

### Step 4: Solve for \( a \)

Combine like terms:
\[
-10 + a = 4 - a
\]

Add \( a \) to both sides:
\[
-10 + 2a = 4
\]

Add 10 to both sides:
\[
2a = 14
\]

Divide by 2:
\[
a = 7
\]

### Conclusion

The value of the constant \( a \) that makes the function \( f(x) \) continuous for all real numbers is \(\boxed{7}\).

문제 5:
To find the value of \( f'(1) \) for the function \( f(x) = (x^2 + 1)(3x^2 - x) \), we need to first find the derivative \( f'(x) \).

The function \( f(x) \) is a product of two functions: \( u(x) = x^2 + 1 \) and \( v(x) = 3x^2 - x \). We will use the product rule to differentiate \( f(x) \), which states:

\[
f'(x) = u'(x)v(x) + u(x)v'(x)
\]

First, we find the derivatives of \( u(x) \) and \( v(x) \):

1. \( u(x) = x^2 + 1 \) implies \( u'(x) = 2x \).

2. \( v(x) = 3x^2 - x \) implies \( v'(x) = 6x - 1 \).

Now, apply the product rule:

\[
f'(x) = u'(x)v(x) + u(x)v'(x) = (2x)(3x^2 - x) + (x^2 + 1)(6x - 1)
\]

We will simplify each term separately:

- For \( u'(x)v(x) = (2x)(3x^2 - x) \):

\[
2x(3x^2 - x) = 2x \cdot 3x^2 - 2x \cdot x = 6x^3 - 2x^2
\]

- For \( u(x)v'(x) = (x^2 + 1)(6x - 1) \):

\[
(x^2 + 1)(6x - 1) = x^2 \cdot 6x + 1 \cdot 6x - x^2 \cdot 1 - 1 \cdot 1 = 6x^3 + 6x - x^2 - 1
\]

Combine these results to find \( f'(x) \):

\[
f'(x) = (6x^3 - 2x^2) + (6x^3 + 6x - x^2 - 1)
\]

Combine like terms:

\[
f'(x) = 6x^3 + 6x^3 - 2x^2 - x^2 + 6x - 1 = 12x^3 - 3x^2 + 6x - 1
\]

Now, we evaluate \( f'(1) \):

\[
f'(1) = 12(1)^3 - 3(1)^2 + 6(1) - 1
\]

Calculate each term:

\[
= 12 \cdot 1 - 3 \cdot 1 + 6 \cdot 1 - 1 = 12 - 3 + 6 - 1
\]

Simplify:

\[
= 14
\]

Thus, the value of \( f'(1) \) is \(\boxed{14}\).

\sin \theta = \frac{1}{5}

\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1

\[
\sin^2 \theta = \left(\frac{1}{5}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{25}

\[
\frac{1}{25} + \cos^2 \theta = 1

\[
\cos^2 \theta = 1 - \frac{1}{25} = \frac{25}{25} - \frac{1}{25} = \frac{24}{25}

\[
\boxed{5}

문제 7:
To solve this problem, we need to determine the function \( f(x) \) given the integral condition:

\[
\int_0^x f(t) \, dt = 3x^3 + 2x
\]

We know from the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus that if

\[
F(x) = \int_0^x f(t) \, dt
\]

then the derivative of \( F(x) \) with respect to \( x \) is

\[
F'(x) = f(x)
\]

Given that

\[
F(x) = 3x^3 + 2x
\]

we differentiate \( F(x) \) to find \( f(x) \):

\[
F'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(3x^3 + 2x) = 9x^2 + 2
\]

Thus, the function \( f(x) \) is

\[
f(x) = 9x^2 + 2
\]

We are asked to find the value of \( f(1) \). Substituting \( x = 1 \) into the expression for \( f(x) \), we get:

\[
f(1) = 9(1)^2 + 2 = 9 \times 1 + 2 = 9 + 2 = 11
\]

Therefore, the value of \( f(1) \) is \(\boxed{11}\).

문제 8:
To solve the problem, we need to evaluate the expression \( a \times b \) where:

\[
a = 2 \log \frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} + \log_2 20
\]
\[
b = \log 2
\]

Let's start by simplifying \( a \).

### Step 1: Simplify \( 2 \log \frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} \)

Using the logarithm power rule, \( \log \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} \right) = \log 10^{-\frac{1}{2}} = -\frac{1}{2} \log 10 \).

Thus,
\[
2 \log \frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} = 2 \times -\frac{1}{2} \log 10 = -\log 10
\]

### Step 2: Simplify \( \log_2 20 \)

We can express 20 as \( 2^2 \times 5 \). Therefore,
\[
\log_2 20 = \log_2 (2^2 \times 5) = \log_2 2^2 + \log_2 5 = 2 + \log_2 5
\]

### Step 3: Combine the expressions for \( a \)

Now substitute the simplified expressions back into the expression for \( a \):
\[
a = -\log 10 + 2 + \log_2 5
\]

### Step 4: Simplify \( a \) further

Recall that \(\log 10 = 1\) because \(\log 10\) is the common logarithm (base 10). Therefore,
\[
a = -1 + 2 + \log_2 5 = 1 + \log_2 5
\]

### Step 5: Calculate \( a \times b \)

Now we have:
\[
b = \log 2
\]

Thus,
\[
a \times b = (1 + \log_2 5) \times \log 2
\]

### Step 6: Use the change of base formula

We use the change of base formula to express \(\log_2 5\) in terms of base 10 logarithms:
\[
\log_2 5 = \frac{\log 5}{\log 2}
\]

Substitute this into the expression for \( a \times b \):
\[
a \times b = (1 + \frac{\log 5}{\log 2}) \times \log 2
\]

Distribute \(\log 2\):
\[
a \times b = \log 2 + \log 5
\]

### Step 7: Simplify the expression

Using the property of logarithms that \(\log a + \log b = \log (a \times b)\), we have:
\[
a \times b = \log (2 \times 5) = \log 10
\]

Since \(\log 10 = 1\), the final answer is:
\[
\boxed{1}
\]

문제 10:
To solve this problem, we need to analyze the function \( f(x) = a \cos(bx) + 3 \) and determine the conditions under which it achieves its maximum value of 13 at \( x = \frac{\pi}{3} \).

### Step 1: Determine the Maximum Condition
The maximum value of the cosine function, \(\cos(bx)\), is 1. Therefore, for \( f(x) \) to have a maximum value of 13, we set:
\[
a \cos(bx) + 3 = 13
\]
\[
a \cdot 1 + 3 = 13
\]
\[
a + 3 = 13
\]
\[
a = 10
\]

### Step 2: Determine the Condition at \( x = \frac{\pi}{3} \)
Since the maximum occurs at \( x = \frac{\pi}{3} \), we have:
\[
\cos\left(b \cdot \frac{\pi}{3}\right) = 1
\]
This implies:
\[
b \cdot \frac{\pi}{3} = 2k\pi \quad \text{for some integer } k
\]
\[
b = 6k
\]

### Step 3: Find the Minimum Value of \( a + b \)
We need \( a \) and \( b \) to be natural numbers, and we have found \( a = 10 \) and \( b = 6k \). To minimize \( a + b \), we choose the smallest natural number \( k = 1 \):
\[
b = 6 \times 1 = 6
\]
Thus, the minimum value of \( a + b \) is:
\[
a + b = 10 + 6 = 16
\]

### Conclusion

문제 11:
To solve this problem, we need to determine the acceleration of point P at the time when its direction of motion changes. The position of point P at time \( t \) is given by:

\[
x(t) = t^3 - \frac{3}{2} t^2 - 6t
\]

### Step 1: Find the velocity function

The velocity \( v(t) \) is the derivative of the position function \( x(t) \) with respect to time \( t \):

\[
v(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(t^3 - \frac{3}{2} t^2 - 6t) = 3t^2 - 3t - 6
\]

### Step 2: Determine when the direction of motion changes

The direction of motion changes when the velocity changes sign, which occurs when \( v(t) = 0 \). Solve for \( t \) in the equation:

\[
3t^2 - 3t - 6 = 0
\]

Divide the entire equation by 3 to simplify:

\[
t^2 - t - 2 = 0
\]

Factor the quadratic equation:

\[
(t - 2)(t + 1) = 0
\]

The solutions are:

\[
t = 2 \quad \text{and} \quad t = -1
\]

Since \( t \geq 0 \), we consider only \( t = 2 \).

### Step 3: Find the acceleration function

The acceleration \( a(t) \) is the derivative of the velocity function \( v(t) \):

\[
a(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(3t^2 - 3t - 6) = 6t - 3
\]

### Step 4: Calculate the acceleration at \( t = 2 \)

Substitute \( t = 2 \) into the acceleration function:

\[
a(2) = 6(2) - 3 = 12 - 3 = 9
\]

Thus, the acceleration of point P at the time when its direction of motion changes is \(\boxed{9}\).

문제 12:

1. **Understanding the sequences:**
   - \( \{a_n\} \) is a sequence with \( a_1 = 2 \).
   - \( \{b_n\} \) is an arithmetic sequence with \( b_1 = 2 \).

   \[
   \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{a_k}{b_{k+1}} = \frac{1}{2} n^2
   \]

3. **Analyzing the arithmetic sequence \( \{b_n\} \):**
   - Since \( b_1 = 2 \) and \( \{b_n\} \) is arithmetic, let the common difference be \( d \).
   - Therefore, \( b_n = 2 + (n-1)d \).
   - Specifically, \( b_{k+1} = 2 + kd \).

4. **Substituting into the condition:**
   \[
   \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{a_k}{2 + kd} = \frac{1}{2} n^2
   \]

5. **Finding a pattern for \( a_k \):**
   - We hypothesize that \( a_k = k \cdot b_{k+1} \) to simplify the sum.
   - This gives \( a_k = k(2 + kd) = 2k + k^2d \).

6. **Verification:**
   - Substitute \( a_k = 2k + k^2d \) into the sum:
   \[
   \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{2k + k^2d}{2 + kd} = \sum_{k=1}^n k = \frac{1}{2} n^2
   \]
   - This simplifies correctly because:
     \[
     \sum_{k=1}^n k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}
     \]
   - For the condition to hold, \( d = 0 \) must be true, as it simplifies the sequence \( \{b_n\} \) to a constant sequence \( b_n = 2 \).

7. **Conclusion:**
   - With \( d = 0 \), \( b_n = 2 \) for all \( n \).
   - Therefore, \( a_k = 2k \).

8. **Calculate \( \sum_{k=1}^5 a_k \):**
   - \( a_1 = 2 \times 1 = 2 \)
   - \( a_2 = 2 \times 2 = 4 \)
   - \( a_3 = 2 \times 3 = 6 \)
   - \( a_4 = 2 \times 4 = 8 \)
   - \( a_5 = 2 \times 5 = 10 \)

9. **Sum:**
   \[
   \sum_{k=1}^5 a_k = 2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + 10 = 30
   \]

Thus, the value of \(\sum_{k=1}^5 a_k\) is \(\boxed{30}\).
