### Key Concepts
1. **Cube Root**: The cube root of a number \(a\) is a number \(b\) such that \(b^3 = a\). It is denoted as \(\sqrt[3]{a}\).
2. **Fractional Exponents**: The expression \(a^{\frac{1}{3}}\) is equivalent to the cube root of \(a\), i.e., \(\sqrt[3]{a}\).
3. **Properties of Exponents**: The product of powers rule states that \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\).

3. **Express 25 as a Power of 5**: Recognize that \(25 = 5^2\), so:
   - \(25^{\frac{1}{3}} = (5^2)^{\frac{1}{3}} = 5^{\frac{2}{3}}\).

   - \(5^{\frac{1}{3}} \times 5^{\frac{2}{3}} = 5^{\frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{3}} = 5^{\frac{3}{3}} = 5^1 = 5\).

### Solution
The value of \(\sqrt[3]{5} \times 25^{\frac{1}{3}}\) is \(5\).

2. **Piecewise Functions:** These are functions defined by different expressions depending on the input value. Ensuring continuity at the boundary points where the expression changes is crucial.
3. **Limit Evaluation:** To ensure continuity at a boundary point, the left-hand limit and the right-hand limit at that point must be equal, and they must also equal the function's value at that point.
